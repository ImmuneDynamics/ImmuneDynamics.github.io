Home

Welcome to the research site of the Immune Dynamics team, a multi-disciplinary collaboration between the Sydney Cytometry Core Research Facility and multiple research and technology labs at the University of Sydney and the Centenary Institute, established as a result of a seed funding grant from the Marie Bashir Institute for Infectious Diseases and Biosecurity. This group combines expertise in immunology, infectious disease, high-dimensional single-cell & imaging technologies, computational biology, and data science, drawing from the Sydney Cytometry Core Research Facility, the School of Medical Sciences, and the School of Computer Science at the University of Sydney. Collectively, our work involves the development of high-dimensional cytometry & imaging methods with the Sydney Cytometry Core Research Facility, as well as the creation of advanced analysis approaches, to address challenges in modern single-cell and imaging sciences. We apply these technologies and approaches collaboratively to the study of immunology and infectious disease, including emerging pathogens such as SARS-CoV-2/COVID-19, Zika virus encephalitis, and West Nile virus encephalitis. This team is led by Dr. Thomas Ashhurst and Dr. Mark Read, with Dr. Felix Marsh-Wakefield, Givanna Putri, Prof. Nicholas King, ... etc.

For more information, check out our team, research, community, and resource pages.
